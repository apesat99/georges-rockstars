George's Story

George O'Shaughnessy was diagnosed with Acute Lymphoblastic Leukaemia in November 2014, aged just 22 months old. George started a 3 and a half year chemotherapy treatment plan but relapsed 2 years into treatment. He then underwent high dose chemotherapy, cranial and total body radiation and a bone marrow transplant, spending many months at a time in hospital, often over a hundred miles from home. Sadly 10 months after his bone marrow transplant George relapsed again and his only option for a cure was to take part in a trial for pioneering CAR T Cell therapy at Great Ormond Street Hospital. After initially responding very well and achieving remission for the third time and finally being given the go ahead to start school a year and a half late, 10 months after his CAR T cell therapy, George's bone marrow devestaingly showed his leukaemia was returning for the fourth time which meant George would not be able to be cured. George spent precious time at home with his family, Mum Amy, Dad Craig and younger sister Bella and passed away at home surrounded by his loving family 4 months later in August 2019. Despite all that life threw at him and spending a huge amount of his short childhood in hospitals, George was well known for his cheeky personality and happy smile. He loved music and hugely benefitted from music therapy while in isolation for his bone marrow transplant at Bristol Children's Hospital. The positive impact of his music therapy sessions led George's parents to set up the charity in his memory to provide music therapy on childrens hospital wards in the UK and improve the lives and experiences of children who often have to spend much of their childhood in hospital.
